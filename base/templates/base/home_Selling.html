Find the right selling option for you ▼

There are several ways to sell your home, each with different benefits:

  • Traditional agent listing: Full service with maximum exposure (typical choice for most sellers)
  • Discount brokers: Lower commission but fewer services
  • iBuyer services: Quick cash offers but usually below market value
  • For Sale By Owner (FSBO): Save on commission but handle everything yourself

Consider your timeline, home condition, and how much involvement you want in the process.

How much can you make selling your home? ▼

Your net profit from selling depends on several factors:

  • Sale price: Based on current market conditions and comparable homes
  • Agent commission: Typically 5-6% of the sale price
  • Closing costs: Usually 1-3% including title insurance, transfer taxes
  • Repairs and staging: Prepare your home to maximize sale price
  • Mortgage payoff: Remaining balance on your loan

On average, sellers walk away with 30-40% of their home's sale price as profit.

Home selling advice

Complete guide to selling your home ▼

The home selling process typically follows these steps:

  • Preparation (1-2 months): Declutter, repair, and stage your home
  • Listing (1 week): Photography, pricing, and MLS listing
  • Marketing (2-8 weeks): Showings, open houses, and negotiations
  • Under contract (30-45 days): Inspections, appraisal, and final preparations
  • Closing: Sign papers and receive your proceeds

The entire process typically takes 3-6 months from start to finish.

Should I sell my home now? ▼

Consider these factors when deciding to sell:

  • Market conditions: Is it a seller's market with low inventory and high demand?
  • Interest rates: Lower rates mean more buyers can afford your home
  • Your equity: Have you built sufficient equity to cover selling costs?
  • Personal timing: Job change, family needs, or lifestyle changes
  • Seasonal factors: Spring and summer typically see more buyers

The best time to sell is when market conditions align with your personal needs.

How much is my home worth? ▼

Your home's value depends on:

  • Location: Neighborhood, school district, and local amenities
  • Size and layout: Square footage, bedrooms, and bathrooms
  • Condition: Age, updates, and maintenance quality
  • Market trends: Current supply and demand in your area
  • Recent sales: What similar homes sold for recently

Work with an agent to get a detailed CMA for the most accurate pricing strategy.

  • Home condition: Is it move-in ready or needs work?
  • Your budget: Can you invest in repairs and staging?

How to select an agent? ▼

Interview multiple agents and ask about:

  • Recent sales: Homes they've sold in your neighborhood in the past year
  • Marketing plan: How they'll showcase your home online and offline
  • Communication: Their availability and preferred contact methods
  • Pricing strategy: How they arrived at their suggested list price
  • References: Speak with recent clients about their experience

Trust your instincts—choose someone you feel comfortable working with for several months.

How to prepare your home for sale? ▼

Maximize your sale price with these preparations:

  • Deep clean: Every room should sparkle, especially kitchens and bathrooms
  • Declutter: Remove personal items and excess furniture
  • Minor repairs: Fix leaky faucets, squeaky doors, and scuffed walls
  • Curb appeal: Landscaping, fresh paint, and welcoming entrance
  • Staging: Arrange furniture to showcase space and flow

Investing 1-3% of your home's value in preparations can yield a 5-10% higher sale price.
